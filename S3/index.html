Hellō: a multi-stakeholder co-operative transforming personal identity.

Online identity today is similar to the world before credit cards. Proving who you are online is tedious and dangerous, just as managing an account at each merchant was tedious, and carrying cash was risky.

A group of innovative banks chose to cooperate to transform global payments by creating the payment interchange, Visa. In the same way, a group of visionary leaders are choosing to cooperate to transform global identity by creating an identity interchange, Hellō.

Visa enabled billions of customers to make simple and safe purchases from millions of merchants. Hellō will give billions of users a simple and safe way to prove who they are to millions of developers.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why is Hellō a cooperative?

    We think it’s crucial that Hellō is mission oriented rather than profit oriented. Cooperatives are owned and controlled by their members.

  • Why is Hellō not a regular corporation?

    Corporations are owned and controlled by shareholders for the purpose of maximizing shareholder value.

  • Why is Hellō not a 501c3 non-profit?

    A 501c3 non-profit lacks ownership and control by the same members who benefit from its services, an important feature to ensure Hellō stays focused on benefiting user and corporate members.

  • Is Hellō a standards organization?

    No, Hellō is not a standards organization. If we require functionality that is not standardized, we will participate in standards organizations such as the OpenID Foundation (OIDF) or the Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF).

  • Who can be a member of Hellō?

    Hellō is a multi-stakeholder cooperative where users, corporations, and employees are members. Any human that signs up and uses Hellō is a user member. Any corporation with an interest in transforming personal identity can join Hellō as a corporate member. All employees are members.

  • What is the governance of Hellō?

    User members elect three directors (1 @ 100M users, 2 @ 500M users, 3 @ 1B users), corporate members elect three directors, and employees elect one director, providing a board of directors with seven members when Hellō has been broadly adopted.
